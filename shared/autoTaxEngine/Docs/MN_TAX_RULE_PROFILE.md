# Minnesota (MN) Vehicle Tax Rule Profile

**State:** Minnesota
**State Code:** MN
**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-14
**Research Date:** 2025-11-14

---

## Executive Summary

Minnesota has a **6.875% state motor vehicle excise tax** (MVET) with **NO local sales taxes** on vehicle **purchases** (state-only system). However, vehicle **leases ARE subject to local sales taxes**, making the lease tax environment significantly different. The state provides **FULL trade-in credit** (no cap), and **BOTH manufacturer AND dealer rebates reduce the taxable price**. Service contracts and GAP are **NOT taxable**. Minnesota uses **UPFRONT lease taxation** on the total lease price, with a unique rule that **cash down payments do NOT reduce the vehicle value for tax**, while **trade-ins DO reduce vehicle value**.

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- State motor vehicle excise tax: 6.875% (effective July 1, 2023)
- PURCHASES: NO local sales tax (state-only 6.875%)
- LEASES: Subject to local sales taxes (can exceed 8% in metro areas)
- Metro transit tax: +0.75% for seven-county Minneapolis-St. Paul area (leases only)
- Trade-in credit: FULL (100% deduction, no cap)
- Manufacturer rebates: NOT taxable (reduce purchase price)
- Dealer rebates: NOT taxable (same as manufacturer)
- Doc fee: NOT taxable (average $75)
- Service contracts/GAP: NOT taxable
- Lease taxation: UPFRONT on total lease price
- Cash down on leases: Does NOT reduce tax base (unique)
- Trade-in on leases: DOES reduce tax base (different from cash)
- Negative equity on leases: NOT taxable

## 1. RETAIL SALES TAX (MOTOR VEHICLE EXCISE TAX)

### 1.1 Tax Rates (Current 2025)

#### State Motor Vehicle Excise Tax (MVET)
- **Rate:** 6.875% (effective July 1, 2023)
- **Previous Rate:** 6.5% (before July 1, 2023)
- **Increase:** 0.375% directed to Greater Minnesota transit
- **Legal Basis:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.02

#### Local Sales Tax on Vehicle Purchases
**NONE** - Local sales taxes do NOT apply to motor vehicle purchases

This is a **unique Minnesota feature**: While local option sales taxes (up to 1.5%) apply to general purchases in many Minnesota jurisdictions, motor vehicles are **explicitly EXEMPT** from local sales taxes.

#### Local Vehicle Excise Tax
**$20 Flat Fee** - Some counties/cities impose flat $20 local vehicle excise tax

This is NOT a sales tax but rather a registration-related fee collected at time of title transfer. Used to fund local transportation projects.

#### Total Rate on Purchases
- **State Only:** 6.875% (consistent statewide)
- **Local Excise:** $20 (if applicable - not all jurisdictions)

**Example Calculation:**
```
Vehicle Purchase Price:        $30,000
Trade-In Value:                -$10,000
Taxable Base:                  $20,000

State MVET (6.875%):           $1,375.00
Local Excise Tax:              $20.00
Total Tax:                     $1,395.00
```

**CRITICAL NOTE:** Motor vehicle PURCHASES are exempt from local sales taxes. Motor vehicle LEASES are NOT exempt and are subject to local sales taxes.

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.02; MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.2 Trade-In Credit Policy

**Policy:** **FULL** - 100% trade-in credit with no cap

#### How It Works
Minnesota provides 100% trade-in credit with no dollar limit. Trade-in value is fully deducted from the purchase price before calculating motor vehicle excise tax.

**Statutory Definition (§ 297B.01, Subd. 14a):**
"If a motor vehicle is taken in trade as a credit or as part payment on a motor vehicle taxable under this chapter, the credit or trade-in value allowed by the person selling the motor vehicle shall be deducted from the total selling price to establish the purchase price of the vehicle being sold."

**Requirements:**
- Trade-in must be a motor vehicle traded for another motor vehicle
- No limit or cap on trade-in credit amount
- Trade-in value allowed by dealer is deducted from total selling price

**Example Calculation:**
```
Vehicle Purchase Price:        $40,000
Trade-In Allowance:            $18,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           $2,500
Taxable Purchase Price:        $19,500

Tax @ 6.875%:                  $1,340.63
```

**EXCEPTION:** Trading an off-road vehicle (ATV, snowmobile, etc.) for a motor vehicle does NOT reduce the taxable price.

**Legal Basis:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.01, Subd. 14; MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.3 Manufacturer Rebate Treatment

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE** - Manufacturer rebates reduce purchase price before tax

#### Tax Treatment
Manufacturer rebates are explicitly excluded from the taxable purchase price.

**Statutory Definition (§ 297B.01, Subd. 14b):**
"Purchase price does not include... amounts representing manufacturer's rebates paid to the purchaser"

**Requirements:**
- Rebate must have fixed value at time of purchase
- Applies to manufacturer rebates paid directly to purchaser
- Applies to manufacturer rebates assigned to dealer on behalf of purchaser
- Third-party rebates also deductible if issued by third parties with manufacturer agreements (e.g., credit card incentive programs)

**Example:**
```
Vehicle MSRP:                  $35,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$3,000
Customer Purchase Price:       $32,000

Taxable Base:                  $32,000 (rebate reduces base)
Tax @ 6.875%:                  $2,200
```

**Federal EV Tax Credits:**
If applied as upfront dealer discount at point of sale: Reduces taxable price
If claimed later on federal tax return: Does NOT reduce taxable price

**Contrast with Other States:**
Unlike Michigan or Colorado where manufacturer rebates are taxable, Minnesota allows rebates to reduce the taxable purchase price, saving customers money.

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.01, Subd. 14; MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.4 Dealer Rebate Treatment

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE** - Dealer incentives also reduce purchase price

Minnesota does NOT distinguish between manufacturer and dealer rebates for tax purposes. Both types reduce the purchase price before tax calculation.

**Key Principle:** Any rebate or discount that reduces the actual consideration paid by the customer reduces the taxable purchase price.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000
Dealer Incentive:              -$1,500
Customer Purchase Price:       $28,500

Taxable Base:                  $28,500
Tax @ 6.875%:                  $1,959.38
```

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.01; MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.5 Documentation Fee (Doc Fee) Taxability and Cap

#### Taxability
**NOT TAXABLE** - Documentation fees are explicitly excluded from taxable purchase price

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide):**
"Tax should not be charged on: Registration, license, and document fees"

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $28,000
Doc Fee:                       $75
Total Customer Cost:           $28,075

Taxable Base (for tax):        $28,000 (doc fee excluded)
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $1,925
```

#### Cap Amount and Regulation

**Statutory Regulation:** Minnesota Statute § 168.27, Subd. 31

**Average Doc Fee:** $75 (Minnesota has one of the lowest average doc fees nationally)

**Recent Legislative Activity:** Legislative discussions have considered caps around $125-$150, but as of 2025, no specific statutory cap is enacted.

**Requirements:**
- Fee must be for services actually rendered
- Services include: preparing, handling, and processing documents for closing
- Must be separately stated on purchase agreement
- Doc fees may be excluded from dealer's advertised price

**Best Practice:** Keep doc fees reasonable and separately itemized to avoid tax issues.

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 168.27, Subd. 31; MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.6 Title Fee Treatment

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE** when separately stated

**Official Guidance:** "Tax should not be charged on: Registration, license, and document fees"

Title fees are government fees and not subject to motor vehicle excise tax.

### 1.7 Service Contract (VSC) Taxability

**Policy:** Service contracts are **NOT subject to motor vehicle sales tax**

#### Extended Warranty / Service Contract Sales

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide):**
"Tax should not be charged on: Extended warranties"

When sold separately from vehicle: May be subject to general sales tax rate of 6.875% (not motor vehicle sales tax).

**Best Practice:** VSC should be separately stated on purchase agreement to ensure non-taxable treatment under motor vehicle excise tax.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000
Service Contract (VSC):        $2,500 (separately stated)
Total Purchase:                $32,500

Taxable Base (MVET):           $30,000 (VSC excluded)
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $2,062.50
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide; Warranties and Service Contracts Guide

### 1.8 GAP Insurance Taxability

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE**

**Official Guidance:** "Do not charge sales tax on: Insurance"

GAP (Guaranteed Asset Protection) is classified as an insurance product and is exempt from Minnesota sales tax.

**Additional Minnesota Requirement:** Minnesota requires insurers to include future sales tax in total-loss settlement checks. This ensures that replacement vehicle purchases account for sales tax.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000
GAP Insurance:                 $895
Total Purchase:                $30,895

Taxable Base (MVET):           $30,000 (GAP excluded)
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $2,062.50
```

### 1.9 Accessories Taxability

**Policy:** **TAXABLE** - All accessories sold with motor vehicle are taxable

Accessories and labor charges for installation are subject to motor vehicle excise tax.

**Examples:**
- Running boards
- Mud flaps
- Cargo liners
- Window tinting
- Remote starters
- Aftermarket wheels
- Audio system upgrades

**Labor Charges:**
Labor for rustproofing, undercoating, dealer prep, and transportation are also taxable.

**EXCEPTION:** Disability modifications are NOT taxable.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $28,000
Accessories (running boards):  $1,200
Installation Labor:            $300
Total Sale Price:              $29,500

Taxable Base:                  $29,500
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $2,028.13
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide

### 1.10 Negative Equity Taxability

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE** (likely, not explicitly addressed)

Minnesota statutes do not explicitly address whether negative equity rolled into a new vehicle loan is subject to sales tax.

#### Legal Analysis

**Purchase Price Definition (§ 297B.01, Subd. 14a):**
"Purchase price means the total consideration valued in money for a sale, whether paid in money or otherwise"

The statute defines purchase price based on:
- The actual sale transaction of the new vehicle
- Minus rebates and trade-in allowances
- Plus taxable add-ons (accessories, labor)

**Conservative Interpretation:**
Negative equity represents a DEBT OBLIGATION from the previous vehicle, not consideration paid for the new vehicle. It's rolled into the loan but is not part of the new vehicle's purchase price.

**Example:**
```
New Vehicle Price:             $30,000
Trade-In Actual Value:         $8,000
Trade-In Payoff:               -$12,000
Negative Equity:               -$4,000

Purchase Price for Tax:        $22,000 (Vehicle price minus trade value)
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $1,512.50

Amount Financed:               $26,000 (includes negative equity)
  Net Vehicle Cost:            $22,000
  Sales Tax:                   $1,512.50
  Negative Equity:             $4,000
  Total Financed:              $27,512.50
```

**Best Practice:** Calculate tax on (New Vehicle Price - Trade-In Allowance). Do NOT add negative equity to taxable base.

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.01; MN DOR guidance analysis

## 2. LEASE TAXATION (UPFRONT ON TOTAL LEASE PRICE)

### 2.1 Lease Tax Method

**Method:** **FULL UPFRONT** - Tax on total lease price calculated and paid upfront

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCE:** Unlike motor vehicle PURCHASES (exempt from local sales tax), motor vehicle LEASES ARE subject to local sales taxes.

**Tax Rate on Leases:**
- State: 6.875%
- Local option taxes: Up to 1.5% (varies by jurisdiction)
- Metro transit tax: +0.75% (seven-county Minneapolis-St. Paul area, effective Oct 1, 2023)
- **Total Range:** 6.875% to 9.125%+ in metro areas

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide):**
"Sales tax is collected on the total up-front lease price and reported on your next Sales and Use Tax return."

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide; Minnesota Statute § 297A.61

### 2.2 Total Lease Price Calculation

#### Formula

```
Total Lease Price =
  Vehicle Capitalized Cost (Cap Cost)
  MINUS: Residual Value
  MINUS: Trade-In Allowance (if any)
  MINUS: Rebates (manufacturer/dealer)
  PLUS: Taxable Add-Ons (accessories installed)
  PLUS: Interest/Finance Charges (money factor cost)
```

**Example:**
```
Vehicle MSRP:                  $40,000
Capitalized Cost:              $38,000
Residual Value (36 months):    $22,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$1,500
Interest/Finance Charges:      $2,500

Total Lease Price:             $17,000
Tax @ 7.375% (state + local):  $1,253.75 (due upfront)
```

#### Financing the Tax

Customers may roll the upfront tax back into the capitalized cost and finance it over the lease term.

**Important:** If the tax is financed:
- Do NOT include the finance charge on the tax in the taxable amount
- Only the original tax amount is taxable

### 2.3 Cap Cost Reduction - UNIQUE MINNESOTA RULE

**Policy:** Cash down payments do NOT reduce vehicle value for tax (UNIQUE)

**CRITICAL UNDERSTANDING:** This is a unique and often misunderstood aspect of Minnesota lease taxation.

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide):**
"Cash down payments or capitalized cost reductions do not reduce the vehicle value."

#### What is Cap Cost Reduction?
- Cash down payment
- Credits or rebates applied at signing (NOT manufacturer rebates)
- First month's payment paid upfront
- Security deposit (non-refundable portion)

#### Tax Treatment
- Cap cost reduction DOES lower monthly lease payments
- Cap cost reduction DOES NOT lower the taxable lease price for tax

**EXCEPTION - Rebates:**
If the cap cost reduction includes manufacturer rebates, those rebates DO reduce the vehicle value (see rebate section below).

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Cap Cost:              $35,000
Cap Cost Reduction (cash):     $3,000
Adjusted Cap Cost:             $32,000
Residual Value:                $20,000
Interest Charges:              $2,000

Total Lease Price for Tax:     $17,000
  ($35,000 - $20,000 + $2,000)
  Note: Cash down does NOT reduce this

MVET @ 6.875%:                 $1,168.75
```

**Why This Matters:**
In many states, a larger down payment reduces the tax burden. In Minnesota, down payments reduce the monthly payment amount but do NOT reduce the total tax paid over the lease.

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide

### 2.4 Rebate Treatment on Leases

**Policy:** **ALWAYS_NON_TAXABLE** - Manufacturer AND dealer rebates reduce vehicle value

In Minnesota, both manufacturer AND dealer rebates reduce the cap cost and are non-taxable, consistent with retail treatment.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Cap Cost:              $38,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$2,500
Adjusted Cap Cost:             $35,500
Residual Value:                $22,000
Interest Charges:              $2,500

Total Lease Price:             $16,000
  ($35,500 - $22,000 + $2,500)

MVET @ 6.875%:                 $1,100
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide

### 2.5 Trade-In Credit on Leases - DIFFERENT from Cash Down

**Policy:** **FULL** - Trade-in allowance DOES reduce vehicle value (different from cash down)

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCE:** Unlike cash down payments, trading in a vehicle DOES reduce the taxable lease price.

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide):**
"Trade-in allowance reduces the vehicle value when the lessor accepts a used vehicle as part of the lease transaction."

#### Requirements
1. Customer must OWN the trade-in (cannot trade-in a leased vehicle)
2. Trade directly to lessor named on the lease agreement
3. Trade-in value is determined by dealer/lessor (not payoff amount)

#### Payoff Amount Rule

**"The payoff amount to a lender does not reduce the trade-in allowance."**

This means if you owe more than the vehicle is worth (negative equity):
- Tax benefit is based on actual trade-in VALUE, not payoff
- Negative equity does not increase the taxable lease price
- Positive equity reduces the taxable lease price

**Example - Positive Equity:**
```
Vehicle Cap Cost:              $35,000
Trade-In Value:                $8,000 (Reduces taxable price)
Trade-In Payoff:               $5,000 (Dealer pays this, not relevant to tax)
Residual Value:                $20,000
Interest Charges:              $2,000

Total Lease Price:             $9,000
  ($35,000 - $8,000 - $20,000 + $2,000)

MVET @ 6.875%:                 $618.75

Without trade-in:
  Total Lease Price: $17,000
  MVET: $1,168.75
  Savings: $550
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide

### 2.6 Negative Equity on Leases

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE**

Negative equity rolled into a lease is NOT taxable.

The tax is calculated based on the actual trade-in VALUE (which reduces the taxable lease price), not the payoff amount.

**Example - Negative Equity:**
```
Vehicle Cap Cost:              $35,000
Trade-In Value:                $6,000 (Reduces taxable price)
Trade-In Payoff:               -$10,000 (Negative equity rolled into cap cost)
Effective Cap Cost:            $39,000 (For payment calculation)
Residual Value:                $20,000
Interest Charges:              $2,000

Total Lease Price (for tax):   $17,000
  ($35,000 - $6,000 - $20,000 + $2,000)
  Note: Based on $6K trade value, NOT $10K payoff

MVET @ 6.875%:                 $1,168.75
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide

### 2.7 Documentation Fee on Leases

**Policy:** **NEVER TAXABLE**

Doc fees on leases are NOT taxable (same as retail).

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide):**
"Do not charge sales tax on: Acquisition, document, title, and registration fees"

**Acquisition Fee vs. Documentation Fee:**
- Acquisition Fee: Charged by lessor (captive finance) - NOT taxable
- Doc Fee: Charged by dealer for administrative processing - NOT taxable
- Both must be separately stated on lease agreement

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide; § 168.27, Subd. 31

### 2.8 Fee Taxability on Leases

**Policy:** Backend products NOT taxable if separately stated

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide):**
"Do not charge sales tax on: Acquisition, document, title, and registration fees; Insurance; Warranty or extended warranty contracts"

#### What's EXCLUDED from Lease Tax:
- GAP insurance/waiver
- Vehicle Service Contracts (VSC)
- Maintenance plans
- Tire & wheel protection
- Key replacement coverage
- Windshield protection
- Paintless dent repair plans

#### What IS INCLUDED in Lease Tax:
- Accessories installed on vehicle (running boards, tint, etc.)
- Labor charges for customization
- Appearance packages
- Technology upgrades installed before delivery

**Best Practice:**
- Separately itemize backend products on lease agreement
- Show VSC/GAP as non-taxable line items
- Calculate tax only on vehicle lease price + taxable add-ons

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Leases Guide

### 2.9 Metro Transit Tax on Leases

**Policy:** +0.75% additional tax for seven-county Minneapolis-St. Paul area

**Effective Date:** October 1, 2023

**Applies To:** Motor vehicle LEASES only (not purchases)

**Seven-County Metro Area:**
1. Hennepin County
2. Ramsey County
3. Dakota County
4. Anoka County
5. Washington County
6. Scott County
7. Carver County

**Example:**
```
Metro Area Lease:
  State MVET: 6.875%
  Local Tax: 0.5%
  Metro Transit: 0.75%
  Total: 8.125%

Total Lease Price:             $15,000
Tax @ 8.125%:                  $1,218.75
```

**Source:** Metropolitan Council - Regional Transportation Sales and Use Tax

## 3. RECIPROCITY AND TAX CREDITS

### 3.1 Credit for Taxes Paid in Other States

**Policy:** Minnesota provides **LIMITED RECIPROCITY** - credit for taxes paid to other states

**Official Guidance (MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide):**
"If a Minnesota resident registered and paid sales tax to another state, Minnesota allows credit for tax paid to the other state. If the other state's tax rate is lower, then tax is due on the difference in rates to Minnesota."

#### How It Works

**Scenario 1: Other State Rate Lower**
```
Vehicle purchased in North Dakota
Purchase Price:                $25,000
North Dakota Sales Tax (5%):   $1,250 (Paid to ND)

Minnesota Tax Due:             $25,000 × 6.875% = $1,718.75
Credit for ND Tax:             -$1,250
Amount Due to Minnesota:       $468.75
```

**Scenario 2: Other State Rate Higher or Equal**
```
Vehicle purchased in Wisconsin
Purchase Price:                $25,000
Wisconsin Sales Tax (5%):      $1,250 (Paid to WI)

Minnesota Tax Due:             $1,718.75
Credit for WI Tax:             $1,250 (full credit)
Amount Due to Minnesota:       $468.75
```

**Scenario 3: No Sales Tax State**
```
Vehicle purchased in Montana (no sales tax)
Purchase Price:                $25,000
Montana Sales Tax:             $0

Minnesota Tax Due:             $1,718.75
Credit for Montana Tax:        $0
Amount Due to Minnesota:       $1,718.75
```

### 3.2 60-Day Residency Rule (Exemption)

**Policy:** Exemption for vehicles purchased while living in another state

**Statute:** § 297B.03, Subd. 3(a)

**Exemption:** "Vehicles purchased by persons who were residents of another state or country more than 60 days prior to becoming Minnesota residents and registered the vehicle in that state or country"

**Practical Application:**
```
Scenario 1: Lived in Wisconsin 5 years, bought car there, moved to MN 3 months later
  Result: NO Minnesota tax due

Scenario 2: Bought car in Wisconsin while living in Minnesota
  Result: Minnesota tax due when registered
```

**Source:** MN DOR Motor Vehicle Sales Guide; Minnesota Statute § 297B.02, § 297B.03

### 3.3 Documentation Required

To receive reciprocity credit:
- Proof of sales tax payment to other state
- Bill of sale or purchase agreement
- Tax receipt from other state DMV/DOR

## 4. UNIQUE MINNESOTA FEATURES

### 4.1 Purchases vs. Leases - Local Tax Treatment

**Feature:** Purchases exempt from local tax, leases subject to local tax

| Transaction | State Tax | Local Tax | Total Range |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Purchase** | 6.875% | NONE | 6.875% |
| **Lease** | 6.875% | 0% - 1.5%+ | 6.875% - 9.125%+ |

**Example:**
```
Minneapolis (7.375% local sales tax area):

PURCHASE:
  Tax Rate: 6.875% (state only)
  Tax on $30K: $2,062.50

LEASE:
  Tax Rate: 7.375% (state + local)
  Tax on $15K lease price: $1,106.25
```

**Why This Matters:**
Metro area lessees can pay 1-2% higher tax rates than buyers, significantly impacting lease affordability.

### 4.2 Cap Cost Reduction Does NOT Reduce Tax (Leases)

**Feature:** Cash down does NOT reduce vehicle value for tax on leases

**How It Differs:**
```
Most States:
  $5,000 down payment reduces lease tax base
  Lower tax due

Minnesota:
  $5,000 down payment does NOT reduce lease tax base
  Same tax as $0 down
```

**Impact:**
```
Lease Price: $18,000
Tax @ 6.875%: $1,237.50

With $5,000 down (most states):
  Adjusted Lease Price: $13,000
  Tax @ 6.875%: $893.75
  Savings: $343.75

With $5,000 down (Minnesota):
  Lease Price: $18,000 (unchanged)
  Tax @ 6.875%: $1,237.50
  Savings: $0
```

### 4.3 Trade-In DOES Reduce Tax on Leases

**Feature:** Unlike cash down, trade-ins DO reduce vehicle value on leases

**Comparison:**
| Reduction Type | Retail | Lease |
|----------------|--------|-------|
| Cash Down | N/A | Does NOT reduce tax |
| Trade-In | Reduces tax | DOES reduce tax |

**Example:**
```
$8,000 Trade-In on Lease:
  Reduces lease price by $8,000
  Tax savings: $8,000 × 6.875% = $550
```

### 4.4 Both Manufacturer and Dealer Rebates Non-Taxable

**Feature:** Minnesota treats ALL rebates as non-taxable

Unlike states like Michigan (rebates taxable) or Colorado (manufacturer rebates taxable), Minnesota provides consistent treatment: ALL rebates reduce taxable price.

### 4.5 Low Doc Fee Average ($75)

**Feature:** Minnesota has one of the lowest average doc fees nationally

**Comparison:**
```
Minnesota Average: $75
Michigan Average: $200+
Colorado Average: $490
National Average: $400+
```

### 4.6 Metro Transit Tax on Leases Only

**Feature:** Seven-county metro area has +0.75% tax on leases only (not purchases)

**Impact:**
```
Metro Area Purchase: 6.875%
Metro Area Lease: 7.625%+ (0.75% higher)
```

### 4.7 Special Rates for Older Vehicles

**Feature:** Flat tax rates for qualifying older vehicles

**$10 Flat Tax:**
- Vehicles 10+ years old
- Below average trade-in value for year/model

**$150 Flat Tax:**
- Vehicles 20+ years old
- Exhibition use (collector vehicles)

**Source:** Minnesota Statute § 297B.025, § 297B.035

### 4.8 Separate Motor Vehicle Excise Tax (Property Tax)

**Feature:** Annual 2.5% property tax on depreciated vehicle value

**CRITICAL:** This is SEPARATE from the one-time motor vehicle sales tax (MVET). It's an annual property tax paid to city/town.

**Example:**
```
One-Time Sales Tax: 6.875% at purchase
Annual Property Tax: 2.5% of depreciated value (ongoing)
```

### 6.1 Required Data Points

**Vehicle Transaction Data:**
- [ ] Vehicle purchase/lease price
- [ ] Trade-in value
- [ ] Trade-in payoff amount
- [ ] Negative equity (if applicable)
- [ ] Manufacturer rebates
- [ ] Dealer rebates/incentives
- [ ] Dealer-installed accessories
- [ ] Documentation fee
- [ ] Title fee
- [ ] Vehicle age (for older vehicle flat tax rates)

**Location Data:**
- [ ] Transaction location (county)
- [ ] Local sales tax rate (for leases)
- [ ] Metro transit tax applicability (seven-county area, leases only)

**Transaction Type:**
- [ ] Purchase vs. Lease
- [ ] New vs. Used vehicle
- [ ] Vehicle age and type (for special rates)

**For Leases:**
- [ ] Capitalized cost
- [ ] Residual value
- [ ] Cap cost reduction (cash down)
- [ ] Trade-in value
- [ ] Interest/finance charges
- [ ] Manufacturer/dealer rebates

**Backend Products:**
- [ ] Service contracts (VSC)
- [ ] GAP insurance
- [ ] Other optional products

### 6.2 Calculation Logic

#### RETAIL PURCHASE CALCULATION:

```
STEP 1: Calculate Base Purchase Price
  Vehicle_Price
  + Accessories
  + Installation_Labor
  - Trade_In_Value
  - Manufacturer_Rebates
  - Dealer_Rebates
  = Taxable_Base

STEP 2: Calculate State MVET
  State_Tax = Taxable_Base × 0.06875

STEP 3: Add Local Excise Tax (if applicable)
  Local_Excise = $20 (flat fee, some jurisdictions)

STEP 4: Calculate Total Tax
  Total_Tax = State_Tax + Local_Excise

STEP 5: Exclude Non-Taxable Items
  Doc_Fee_Tax = $0
  Title_Fee_Tax = $0
  VSC_Tax = $0
  GAP_Tax = $0

STEP 6: Calculate Total Amount Due
  Total_Due = Taxable_Base + Total_Tax + Doc_Fee + Title_Fee + VSC + GAP
```

**Example Implementation:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $32,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$2,500
Trade-In Value:                -$8,000
Accessories:                   $1,500
Doc Fee:                       $75 (NOT taxed)
Service Contract:              $2,500 (NOT taxed)
GAP:                           $895 (NOT taxed)

Taxable Base:
  $32,000 - $2,500 - $8,000 + $1,500 = $23,000

State MVET (6.875%):           $1,581.25
Local Excise:                  $20.00
Total Tax:                     $1,601.25

Total Amount Due:
  Taxable Base:                $23,000.00
  Tax:                         $1,601.25
  Doc Fee:                     $75.00
  VSC:                         $2,500.00
  GAP:                         $895.00
  Total:                       $28,071.25
```

#### LEASE CALCULATION:

```
STEP 1: Calculate Total Lease Price
  Base_Cap_Cost
  - Residual_Value
  - Trade_In_Value  (reduces lease price)
  - Manufacturer_Rebates
  - Dealer_Rebates
  + Interest_Charges
  + Accessories
  = Total_Lease_Price

  NOTE: Cash down (cap reduction) does NOT reduce lease price

STEP 2: Determine Tax Rate
  State_Rate = 0.06875
  + Local_Rate (varies, 0% to 1.5%)
  + Metro_Transit_Rate (0.75% if seven-county area)
  = Total_Tax_Rate

STEP 3: Calculate Upfront Tax
  Upfront_Tax = Total_Lease_Price × Total_Tax_Rate

STEP 4: Add Non-Taxable Items
  Service contracts, GAP, doc fee, acquisition fee, title fee

STEP 5: Calculate Due at Signing
  Due_At_Signing = Cap_Reduction + Upfront_Tax + Non_Taxable_Items
```

**Example Implementation:**
```
MINNEAPOLIS METRO LEASE:

Cap Cost:                      $38,000
Cash Down (cap reduction):     $3,000 (does NOT reduce tax base)
Trade-In Value:                $5,000 (DOES reduce tax base)
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$1,500
Residual Value:                $22,000
Interest Charges:              $2,500
Service Contract:              $1,800
GAP:                           $695
Doc Fee:                       $75

Total Lease Price (for tax):
  $38,000 - $22,000 - $5,000 - $1,500 + $2,500 = $12,000

Tax Rate (Minneapolis metro):
  State: 6.875%
  Local: 0.5%
  Metro Transit: 0.75%
  Total: 8.125%

Upfront Tax:                   $12,000 × 8.125% = $975.00

Due at Signing:
  Cash Down:                   $3,000.00
  Upfront Tax:                 $975.00
  Service Contract:            $1,800.00 (NOT taxed)
  GAP:                         $695.00 (NOT taxed)
  Doc Fee:                     $75.00 (NOT taxed)
  Total:                       $6,545.00
```

### 6.3 Edge Cases and Special Scenarios

```
Regular Rate: $8,000 × 6.875% = $550

Customer Qualifies for: $10 flat tax
Savings: $540
```

#### Edge Case 2: Collector Vehicle
```
Vehicle: 1985 classic car (40 years old)
Use: Exhibition only
Value: $25,000

Special Rate: $150 flat tax
Regular Rate: $25,000 × 6.875% = $1,718.75

Customer Qualifies for: $150 flat tax
Savings: $1,568.75
```

#### Edge Case 3: Large Manufacturer Rebate
```
Vehicle MSRP:                  $45,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$10,000
Customer Price:                $35,000

Taxable Base:                  $35,000 (rebate reduces base)
MVET @ 6.875%:                 $2,406.25

If rebate were taxable (like Michigan):
  Tax: $45,000 × 6.875% = $3,093.75
  Savings in MN: $687.50
```

#### Edge Case 4: Metro Lease vs. Purchase
```
Vehicle: $40,000
Location: Hennepin County (metro area)

PURCHASE:
  Tax Rate: 6.875% (state only)
  Tax: $2,750

LEASE (total lease price $18,000):
  Tax Rate: 8.125% (state + local + metro transit)
  Tax: $1,462.50
```

#### Edge Case 5: Cash Down vs. Trade-In on Lease
```
Scenario 1: $6,000 Cash Down
  Lease Price: $15,000 (cash down does NOT reduce)
  Tax @ 6.875%: $1,031.25

Scenario 2: $6,000 Trade-In
  Lease Price: $9,000 (trade-in DOES reduce)
  Tax @ 6.875%: $618.75

Difference: $412.50 savings with trade-in
```

### 6.4 Validation Rules

**System should validate/flag:**

1. **Rebate Added to Tax Base**
   - Error: "Manufacturer and dealer rebates reduce purchase price in Minnesota"

2. **Cash Down Reduces Lease Tax Base**
   - Error: "Cash down does NOT reduce vehicle value for tax on leases in Minnesota"

3. **Trade-In Doesn't Reduce Lease Tax Base**
   - Error: "Trade-in DOES reduce vehicle value for tax on leases in Minnesota"

4. **Doc Fee Taxed**
   - Error: "Doc fees are NOT taxable in Minnesota"

5. **Service Contracts or GAP Taxed**
   - Error: "VSC and GAP are NOT taxable if optional and separately stated"

6. **Local Tax on Purchase**
   - Warning: "Motor vehicle purchases are exempt from local sales tax in Minnesota"

7. **Metro Transit Tax on Purchase**
   - Error: "Metro transit tax applies to LEASES only, not purchases"

8. **Negative Equity on Lease Increases Tax Base**
   - Info: "Negative equity does NOT increase lease tax base. Tax based on trade-in VALUE, not payoff."
