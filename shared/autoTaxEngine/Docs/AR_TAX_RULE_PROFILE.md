# Arkansas (AR) Automotive Sales and Lease Taxation Profile

**State:** Arkansas
**State Code:** AR
**Document Version:** 1.0
**Last Updated:** 2025-11-14
**Research Date:** 2025-11-14

---

## Executive Summary

Arkansas imposes a **6.5% state sales tax** on vehicle purchases (with reduced rates for lower-priced vehicles), combined with local taxes averaging 2.688%. The state provides **full trade-in credit** for retail purchases within a 60-day window but **NO trade-in credit for leases**. Arkansas is unique in that it **TAXES both service contracts and GAP insurance**, unlike most states. The state has **NO reciprocity** for out-of-state vehicle taxes - Arkansas residents must pay full Arkansas tax even if they paid tax elsewhere. Leases are taxed monthly with an additional 1.5% long-term rental vehicle tax.

**Key Distinguishing Features:**
- Tiered state tax rates: Exempt under $4,000, 3.5% for $4,000-$10,000, 6.5% for $10,000+
- Full trade-in credit for purchases (60-day window), but NO credit for leases
- Service contracts and GAP are TAXABLE (unusual - most states exempt these)
- NO reciprocity - no credit for taxes paid to other states
- Manufacturer and dealer rebates are TAXABLE
- Lease trade-in deduction explicitly denied by Arkansas Admin Code
- Additional 1.5% long-term rental vehicle tax on lease payments
- Tax paid directly to DFA at registration, not to dealer

## 1. RETAIL SALES TAX

### 1.1 Tax Rates (Current 2025)

#### State Rate - Tiered System
Arkansas uses a tiered tax system based on vehicle value:

| Vehicle Value | State Tax Rate | Notes |
|--------------|----------------|-------|
| Under $4,000 | **0%** | Exempt from state sales tax |
| $4,000 - $9,999 | **3.5%** | Reduced rate |
| $10,000 and above | **6.5%** | Full rate |

**Legal Basis:** Arkansas Code Title 26, Chapter 52 - Gross Receipts Tax

#### Local Rates
**Structure:** State + County + City/District
- **Local Rates Range:** 0% to 5%
- **Average Local Rate:** ~2.688%
- **Total Combined Rates:** Typically 6.5% to 11.5% (state + local)

**Examples by Major Jurisdiction:**
| Location | State | Local | Total | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|-------|-------|
| Little Rock | 6.5% | 2.5% | 9.0% | Pulaski County |
| Fort Smith | 6.5% | 3.0% | 9.5% | Sebastian County |
| Fayetteville | 6.5% | 2.0% | 8.5% | Washington County |
| Bentonville | 6.5% | 2.0% | 8.5% | Washington County |
| Conway | 6.5% | 2.5% | 9.0% | Faulkner County |

**Official Rate Resources:**
- Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration (DFA)
- Local tax rates vary by county and city
- Rates updated regularly; verify current rates before transactions

### 1.2 Trade-In Credit Policy

**Policy:** **FULL** - Trade-in credit applies to both state and local taxes

#### How It Works (60-Day Window)
Arkansas allows full trade-in credit, but with specific timing requirements:
1. Trade-in or sale must occur within **60 days before or after** the purchase
2. Trade-in value is deducted from vehicle purchase price
3. Sales tax calculated on **net difference** (purchase price minus trade-in)
4. Credit applies to BOTH state and local taxes

**Example Calculation:**
```
Vehicle Purchase Price:        $30,000
Trade-In Value:               -$10,000
Net Taxable Amount:            $20,000

State Tax (6.5% on net):       $1,300
Local Tax (2.5% on net):       $500
Total Tax Due:                 $1,800

(vs. $2,700 if no trade-in credit)
```

**Legal Basis:**
- Act 1232 of 1997 as amended by Act 277 of 2021
- Arkansas Admin. Code R. 006.05.08 § 12.1 - Sales tax credit for private sale

**Critical Requirements:**
- Trade-in or sale must be within 60 days of purchase
- Applies to new and used motor vehicles, trailers, semitrailers
- Consumer pays tax only on net difference
- Does NOT apply to leases (explicitly excluded)

### 1.3 Manufacturer Rebate Treatment

**Policy:** **TAXABLE** - Manufacturer rebates do NOT reduce taxable sales price

#### Tax Treatment
- Rebates are applied AFTER tax calculation
- Buyer pays sales tax on full vehicle price before rebates
- No tax reduction for manufacturer incentives or cash-back offers

**Example:**
```
Vehicle MSRP:                  $25,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           -$3,000
Customer Pays:                 $22,000

Tax Base:                      $25,000  (full price, NOT $22,000)
Sales Tax (6.5% state):        $1,625
Local Tax (2.5%):              $625
Total Tax:                     $2,250

Customer Final Payment:        $22,000 + $2,250 = $24,250
```

**Lease Treatment:**
For leases, manufacturer rebates applied as cap cost reduction are also fully taxable.

**Contrast with Trade-Ins:**
Unlike trade-ins (which DO reduce the taxable base), manufacturer rebates provide no tax benefit to the buyer.

### 1.4 Dealer Rebate Treatment

**Policy:** Dealer rebates are TAXABLE (same as manufacturer rebates)

Dealer incentives/rebates are treated similarly to manufacturer rebates:
- Tax calculated on purchase price before dealer rebates
- Customer receives rebate after tax is paid
- No reduction to taxable amount

### 1.5 Documentation Fee (Doc Fee) Taxability and Cap

#### Taxability
**TAXABLE** - Documentation fees are subject to Arkansas sales tax

Doc fees are included in the taxable base for sales tax calculation at both state and local levels.

#### Cap Amount
**NO CAP** - Arkansas does not impose a statutory limit on dealer documentation fees

- **Average Doc Fee:** ~$110 (considerably lower than many states)
- **Observed Range:** $50 to $200
- Dealers may set their own documentation fees
- Doc fees must be disclosed to buyers

**Comparison:** Arkansas doc fees average much lower than states like Florida ($150 cap enforced) or California (higher averages despite cap).

### 1.6 Title Fee Treatment

**Policy:** **NOT TAXABLE** when separately stated

**Current Title Fee:** Varies by county ($10-$15 range)
**Registration Fee:** Varies by vehicle age and type

Government fees for title and registration are NOT subject to sales tax.

### 1.7 Service Contract (VSC) Taxability

**Policy:** Service contracts are **TAXABLE** - Arkansas is UNUSUAL

#### Extended Warranty / Service Contract Sales
Service contracts (extended warranties, vehicle service contracts) sold with vehicles ARE subject to Arkansas sales tax. This is a unique feature of Arkansas tax law.

**Legal Basis:**
- Arkansas Admin. Code R. 006.05.08 § 9: "Services subject to tax"
- Service contracts sold with motor vehicles are subject to sales tax

**How It Works:**
- Customer pays sales tax on BOTH the vehicle AND the service contract
- Tax is paid at time of vehicle registration to DFA
- Once tax is paid on the contract, labor/parts used under the contract are exempt

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $28,000
Service Contract:              +$2,500
Total Taxable:                 $30,500

Sales Tax (9%):                $2,745

(Service contract adds $225 to tax bill)
```

**Related Products:**
- Extended warranties: TAXABLE
- Maintenance plans: TAXABLE
- Tire and wheel protection: TAXABLE

### 1.8 GAP Insurance Taxability

**Policy:** **TAXABLE** - Arkansas is UNUSUAL

"GAP coverage IS taxable in Arkansas"
- Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration guidance

GAP (Guaranteed Asset Protection) is structured in Arkansas as a waiver or addendum to the finance contract rather than traditional insurance, making it subject to sales tax.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $25,000
Service Contract:              $1,800
GAP Coverage:                  $695
Total Taxable:                 $27,495

Sales Tax (9%):                $2,474.55
```

**Critical Note:** This differs from most states where GAP is treated as insurance and exempt from sales tax.

### 1.9 Accessories Taxability

**Policy:** Dealer-installed accessories are TAXABLE

#### Accessories Purchased WITH Vehicle
**Taxable at FULL RATE (state + local)**

- Accessories included in the vehicle sale are taxed at the combined state + local rate
- Includes: aftermarket wheels, running boards, lift kits, audio systems, remote starters, etc.

**Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000
Accessories (wheels, audio):   +$3,000
Total Sale Price:              $33,000
Tax (9%):                      $2,970
```

### 1.10 Negative Equity Taxability

**Policy:** Treatment differs between SALES and LEASES

#### For Vehicle PURCHASES (Sales)
**TAXABLE** - Negative equity rolled into loan increases taxable amount

- Negative equity becomes part of the taxable base
- Sales tax is calculated on vehicle price PLUS negative equity
- Full amount subject to sales tax

**Example:**
```
New Vehicle Price:             $25,000
Trade-In Value:                $15,000
Trade-In Payoff:               -$18,000
Negative Equity:               -$3,000

Tax Base:                      $25,000 + $3,000 = $28,000
                              (or $13,000 after $15k trade credit)
Sales Tax (9% on $13,000):     $1,170

Note: Negative equity increases base, then trade-in credit applies
```

#### For Vehicle LEASES
**TAXABLE** - Negative equity added to lease increases capitalized cost

Negative equity increases the cap cost, which increases monthly payments and thus increases the tax paid on each payment.

## 2. LEASE TAXATION

### 2.1 Lease Tax Method

**Method:** **MONTHLY PAYMENT TAXATION**

Arkansas taxes leases on each periodic (monthly) payment:
1. **Monthly lease payments** are taxed as they are made
2. Tax applies to each payment, not the total amount upfront
3. Lessor has two options (for vehicles leased after August 1, 1997)

**Lessor Options:**
- **Option 1:** Pay sales/use tax on vehicle at registration; collect NO lease taxes from lessee
- **Option 2:** Register vehicle tax-exempt; collect sales tax + 1.5% rental tax on all payments

Most lessors choose Option 2 for cash flow reasons.

### 2.2 Lease Tax Rates

#### State Rates
Arkansas applies TWO taxes to lease payments:
- **State Sales Tax:** 6.5%
- **Long-Term Rental Vehicle Tax:** 1.5% (for rentals 30+ days)
- **Total State Rate:** 8.0%

#### Local Rates
**County and municipal sales taxes also apply** to lease payments
- Local rates: 0% to 5% (average ~2.688%)
- Total combined typical rate: ~10% to 13.5%

**Example (Little Rock - 9% combined):**
```
Monthly Payment:               $450
Sales Tax (6.5%):              $29.25
Long-term Rental Tax (1.5%):   $6.75
Local Tax (2.5%):              $11.25
Total Monthly Tax:             $47.25
Total Monthly Payment:         $497.25
```

### 2.3 Cap Cost Reduction Taxability

**Policy:** Cap cost reductions are **FULLY TAXABLE**

**CRITICAL:** Arkansas Admin Code R. 006.05.08 § 20 explicitly states:
"The trade-in deduction available to purchasers of motor vehicles... shall not apply to leased motor vehicles."

#### What is Taxable
ALL of the following cap cost reductions are subject to sales tax:
- Cash down payments (TAXABLE)
- Manufacturer rebates applied as cap reduction (TAXABLE)
- Trade-in equity (TAXABLE - NO CREDIT)
- Dealer discounts applied as cap reduction (TAXABLE)

**Example:**
```
Vehicle MSRP:                  $40,000
Cap Cost Reduction (cash):     $5,000
Cap Cost Reduction (rebate):   $2,500
Trade-In Equity:               $8,000
Total Cap Cost Reduction:      $15,500

Adjusted Capitalized Cost:     $24,500

All $15,500 in cap reductions are TAXABLE
(No deduction, no credit for any portion)

Monthly Payment:               $400
Tax on Monthly Payment (10%):  $40
Total Monthly:                 $440
```

### 2.4 Trade-In Credit on Leases

**Policy:** **NO TRADE-IN CREDIT** - Trade-ins do NOT reduce tax on leases

"The trade-in deduction available to purchasers of motor vehicles... shall not apply to leased motor vehicles."
- Arkansas Admin. Code R. 006.05.08 § 20

#### Treatment of Trade-Ins on Leases

**If Trade-In Has Positive Equity:**
Trade-in equity applied as cap cost reduction is FULLY TAXABLE with NO credit or deduction.

**If Trade-In Has Negative Equity:**
Negative equity increases the capitalized cost and is TAXABLE through higher monthly payments.

**Critical Difference from Sales:**
This is a major disadvantage of leasing vs. purchasing in Arkansas:
- **Purchase:** Trade-in reduces taxable amount (saves 9% on trade value)
- **Lease:** Trade-in provides NO tax benefit; fully taxable

**Example Comparison:**
```
PURCHASE SCENARIO:
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000
Trade-In Value:                $10,000
Net Taxable:                   $20,000
Sales Tax (9%):                $1,800
Tax Savings from Trade-In:     $900

LEASE SCENARIO:
Cap Cost:                      $30,000
Trade-In Equity:               $10,000
Cap Reduction (taxable):       $10,000
Monthly Payment:               $350
Monthly Tax (10%):             $35
Trade-In Tax Benefit:          $0 (NONE)
```

### 2.5 Backend Product Taxability on Leases

**Policy:** Backend products on leases follow same rules as sales

#### Service Contracts (VSC)
**TAXABLE**
- Service contracts are subject to sales tax on leases
- Same as retail transactions

#### GAP Insurance
**TAXABLE**
- GAP insurance is subject to sales tax on leases
- Structured as waiver/addendum, subject to tax

#### Accessories
**TAXABLE**
- Accessories included in lease agreement are subject to sales tax
- Tax applies to portion of monthly payment attributable to accessories

### 2.6 Documentation Fee Treatment on Leases

**Policy:** Doc fees are **TAXABLE** on lease transactions

Dealer documentation fees, processing fees, and similar charges are subject to sales tax when included in lease transactions.

## 3. RECIPROCITY AND TAX CREDITS

### 3.1 Credit for Taxes Paid in Other States

**Policy:** Arkansas provides **NO RECIPROCAL CREDIT** for taxes paid to other states

"Arkansas does NOT provide credit for sales or use taxes paid to another state for motor vehicles, trailers, or semitrailers that are first registered by the purchaser in Arkansas."
- Arkansas Department of Finance and Administration

#### Legal Framework
Arkansas Code § 26-52-510 and related provisions:
- No credit for out-of-state taxes paid
- Arkansas residents buying vehicles out-of-state pay full AR tax
- Proof of prior state taxes paid does not reduce AR tax obligation
- Other states reciprocally deny credit for AR taxes

#### How It Works

**Scenario 1: Out-of-State Purchase**
Arkansas resident buys vehicle in another state and brings it to Arkansas:

**Example:**
```
Vehicle purchased in Tennessee:   $30,000
Tennessee tax paid (7%):          $2,100
Arkansas tax due (9%):            $2,700
Credit allowed:                   $0
Additional AR tax due:            $2,700 (full amount)

Total taxes paid:                 $4,800 (both states, no offset)
```

**Scenario 2: In-State Trade-In Credit**
The ONLY credits available are in-state transactions:
- Trade-in credit: Within 60-day window for purchase transactions
- Private sale credit: Sell used vehicle, buy new/used within 60 days

### 3.2 Special Rules and Limitations

#### No Credit Regardless of Other State's Tax Rate
**Arkansas does NOT provide credit even if:**
- You paid higher tax in another state
- You have receipts proving tax payment
- The other state would provide credit for Arkansas taxes

This is a reciprocal denial policy - states that don't recognize Arkansas taxes, Arkansas doesn't recognize theirs either.

#### Documentation Cannot Help
Unlike states with reciprocity, providing documentation of taxes paid elsewhere does NOT reduce Arkansas tax liability.

#### In-State Credits Only
Available credits (in-state transactions only):
- **Trade-in credit:** Within 60-day window for purchases
- **Private sale credit:** Sell used vehicle, buy new/used within 60 days
- Both credits apply ONLY to Arkansas transactions

## 4. UNIQUE STATE FEATURES

### 4.1 Tiered State Tax Rate System

**Feature:** Arkansas has THREE state tax rates based on vehicle value

| Vehicle Value | Rate | Purpose |
|--------------|------|---------|
| Under $4,000 | 0% (exempt) | Affordability for lower-income buyers |
| $4,000 - $9,999 | 3.5% (reduced) | Transitional rate |
| $10,000+ | 6.5% (full) | Standard vehicle tax rate |

**Example Impact:**
```
$3,500 Vehicle:
State Tax:                     $0 (exempt)
Local Tax (2.5%):              $87.50
Total Tax:                     $87.50

$8,000 Vehicle:
State Tax (3.5%):              $280
Local Tax (2.5%):              $200
Total Tax:                     $480

$25,000 Vehicle:
State Tax (6.5%):              $1,625
Local Tax (2.5%):              $625
Total Tax:                     $2,250
```

### 4.2 Service Contracts and GAP Are TAXABLE

**Feature:** Arkansas taxes F&I products that most states exempt

Most states treat service contracts and GAP as insurance products (exempt), but Arkansas treats them as taxable gross receipts.

**Impact Example:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $28,000
Service Contract:              $2,500
GAP Coverage:                  $695
Total Taxable:                 $31,195

Sales Tax (9%):                $2,807.55

F&I products add $287.55 to tax bill
```

**Why Notable:** Only a handful of states tax these products. Arkansas is among the most aggressive in taxing F&I.

### 4.3 No Lease Trade-In Credit (Explicitly Denied)

**Feature:** Arkansas explicitly denies trade-in credit for leases

Arkansas Admin Code R. 006.05.08 § 20 makes it clear:
"The trade-in deduction available to purchasers of motor vehicles... shall not apply to leased motor vehicles."

**Impact:**
```
PURCHASE with $10,000 trade-in:
Tax savings:                   $900 (9% of $10,000)

LEASE with $10,000 trade-in:
Tax savings:                   $0 (NO CREDIT)
Tax disadvantage of lease:     $900
```

### 4.4 Dual Lease Tax System (1.5% Additional)

**Feature:** Arkansas adds 1.5% long-term rental vehicle tax to leases

- Standard sales tax: 6.5% state + local
- Long-term rental tax: 1.5% additional
- Combined: ~8% state + local rates

**Example (10% combined):**
```
Monthly Payment:               $500
Sales Tax (6.5% + 2.5%):       $45
Long-term Rental Tax (1.5%):   $7.50
Total Monthly Tax:             $52.50
Total Monthly:                 $552.50

Over 36 months:                $1,890 in taxes
```

### 4.5 No Reciprocity (Double Taxation Possible)

**Feature:** No credit for out-of-state taxes creates potential double taxation

**Example:**
```
Arkansas resident buys in Texas:
Texas sales tax (6.25%):       $1,875 on $30,000 vehicle
Arkansas sales tax (9%):       $2,700 on $30,000 vehicle
Total taxes paid:              $4,575 (15.25% effective rate)

No offset, no credit, both states collect full tax
```

**Why Notable:** Most states provide at least partial credit. Arkansas's no-reciprocity policy can result in effective tax rates exceeding 15%.

### 4.6 Tax Paid at Registration (Not to Dealer)

**Feature:** Arkansas uses direct payment to DFA

- Tax is NOT collected by dealer at sale
- Customer pays tax directly to Department of Finance and Administration (DFA)
- Payment occurs at time of vehicle registration
- This differs from most states where dealers collect and remit taxes

**Process:**
1. Customer buys vehicle from dealer
2. Customer takes paperwork to DFA office for registration
3. Customer pays sales tax directly to DFA at registration
4. DFA issues title and registration

## 8. IMPLEMENTATION NOTES FOR TAX CALCULATION SYSTEM

### 8.1 Required Data Points

To correctly calculate Arkansas automotive sales tax, the system must capture:

**Vehicle Transaction Data:**
- [ ] Vehicle sale price (gross amount)
- [ ] Trade-in value (if applicable)
- [ ] Trade-in date (verify 60-day window)
- [ ] Trade-in payoff amount (if applicable)
- [ ] Negative equity from trade-in (if applicable)
- [ ] Manufacturer rebates (amount and type)
- [ ] Dealer discounts/rebates
- [ ] Accessories included in sale
- [ ] Documentation fee amount
- [ ] Service contract amount (TAXABLE)
- [ ] GAP coverage amount (TAXABLE)
- [ ] Title fee (not taxable)
- [ ] Registration fee (not taxable)

**Location Data:**
- [ ] County of registration (determines local tax rate)
- [ ] State tax rate tier (based on vehicle value)
- [ ] Local tax rate for county
- [ ] Combined total rate

**Transaction Type:**
- [ ] Purchase vs. Lease
- [ ] New vs. Used vehicle
- [ ] Out-of-state purchase (no credit available)

**For Leases:**
- [ ] Capitalized cost
- [ ] Cap cost reductions (itemized - all taxable)
- [ ] Trade-in equity applied (NO CREDIT)
- [ ] Monthly lease payment amount
- [ ] Lease term (months)

### 8.2 Calculation Logic

#### PURCHASE (RETAIL SALE) CALCULATION:

```
STEP 1: Determine State Tax Rate Based on Vehicle Value
  If vehicle_price < $4,000:
    state_rate = 0% (exempt)
  Else if vehicle_price < $10,000:
    state_rate = 3.5%
  Else:
    state_rate = 6.5%

STEP 2: Calculate Taxable Base
  Taxable_Base = Vehicle_Price
                + Accessories
                + Doc_Fee
                + Service_Contract   (TAXABLE in AR)
                + GAP_Coverage       (TAXABLE in AR)
                + Negative_Equity
                - Trade_In_Value     (if within 60 days)
                - Manufacturer_Rebates (NOT DEDUCTED - taxable)

STEP 3: Calculate State Tax
  State_Tax = Taxable_Base × state_rate

STEP 4: Calculate Local Tax
  Local_Tax = Taxable_Base × local_rate

STEP 5: Calculate Total Sales Tax
  Total_Tax = State_Tax + Local_Tax

STEP 6: Calculate Total Amount Due
  Total_Due = Taxable_Base + Total_Tax
```

**Example Implementation:**
```
Vehicle Price:                 $28,000
Service Contract:              $2,500
GAP Coverage:                  $695
Doc Fee:                       $110
Trade-In Value:                -$10,000
Manufacturer Rebate:           (not deducted)

Taxable Base:                  $21,305
  ($28,000 + $2,500 + $695 + $110 - $10,000)

State Tax (6.5%):              $1,384.83
Local Tax (2.5%):              $532.63
Total Tax:                     $1,917.46

Total Due:                     $23,222.46
(Plus $3,000 rebate applied after = $20,222.46 final)
```

#### LEASE CALCULATION:

```
STEP 1: Calculate Tax on Cap Cost Reduction (All Taxable)
  Cap_Reduction_Total = Cash_Down
                      + Manufacturer_Rebates
                      + Trade_In_Equity    (NO CREDIT)
                      + Other_Reductions

  No deductions or credits available for leases

STEP 2: Calculate Monthly Payment Tax
  Monthly_Sales_Tax = Monthly_Payment × (state_rate + local_rate)
  Monthly_Rental_Tax = Monthly_Payment × 0.015  (1.5% rental tax)
  Total_Monthly_Tax = Monthly_Sales_Tax + Monthly_Rental_Tax

STEP 3: Calculate Total Lease Tax Over Term
  Total_Lease_Tax = (Total_Monthly_Tax × Lease_Term_Months)

STEP 4: Add Taxable Backend Products
  Service_Contract_Tax = Service_Contract × (state_rate + local_rate + 0.015)
  GAP_Tax = GAP × (state_rate + local_rate + 0.015)
```

**Example Implementation:**
```
LEASE TERMS:
Gross Cap Cost:                $35,000
Cash Down:                     $3,000
Trade-In Equity:               $5,000  (NO CREDIT)
Total Cap Reduction:           $8,000
Adjusted Cap Cost:             $27,000
Monthly Payment:               $450
Term:                          36 months
State Rate:                    6.5%
Local Rate:                    2.5%
Rental Tax:                    1.5%

MONTHLY TAX:
Sales Tax: $450 × 9%:          $40.50
Rental Tax: $450 × 1.5%:       $6.75
Total Monthly Tax:             $47.25

Total Monthly Payment:         $497.25

TOTAL LEASE TAX (36 months):
Monthly Tax × 36:              $1,701

Note: Trade-in provides NO tax benefit on leases
```

```
Vehicle Price:                 $30,000

Arkansas tax due (9%):         $2,700
Credit for TX tax:             $0 (no reciprocity)
Total AR tax owed:             $2,700
Total taxes paid:              $4,575 (both states)
```

### 8.4 Validation Rules

**System should validate/flag:**

1. **Trade-In Date Outside 60-Day Window**
   - Error: Trade-in credit only available within 60 days
   - Recalculate with no trade-in credit

2. **Service Contract or GAP Not Taxed**
   - Error: Arkansas REQUIRES tax on service contracts and GAP
   - Add to taxable base

3. **Lease with Trade-In Credit Applied**
   - Error: Arkansas explicitly denies trade-in credit for leases
   - Remove trade-in credit, tax full cap reduction

4. **Manufacturer Rebate Reduces Tax Base**
   - Error: Rebates do NOT reduce Arkansas tax base
   - Recalculate with full price before rebates
